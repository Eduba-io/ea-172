01 / The Eduba frame

Most organizations automate the wrong things.

We work from a simple split. About 60% of what a research-to-production hardware team does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a real AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anyone writes code.

Applied to Impossible Objects, the map resolves fast. Materials-qualification test data, DfAM design libraries, customer onboarding knowledge bases, and defense-capture program-office documentation are database and code problems. ITAR and CUI routing, AS9100 and NADCAP audit trails, CMMC readiness gating, and DoD program-of-record qualification rules are rule-based logic. AI-assisted DfAM advisory and visual anomaly detection on production parts is the 10%.

A layered context architecture over the first two layers gives the engineering team their week back and gives defense customers a documentation posture they can audit without paging the CTO.

03 / The underlying research

The methodology is published.

Interpretable Context Methodology: Folder Structure as Agent Architecture, submitted to ACM TiiS. Co-authored with Microservice Workflow Patterns for distributed-workflow architecture. The core idea is that agent context can be organized as a layered filesystem with measurable gains in interpretability and reproducibility. A process-control plus quality-inspection plus documentation pipeline lives on exactly that architecture. Open source under MIT license.

Repo: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-

Companion: Ethics Engine (arXiv:2510.11742). Psychometric evaluation for LLM ideological and moral patterns. Relevant when an AI layer has to stand up to defense-customer review.
